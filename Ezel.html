Role

Ezel is a type design project focused on creating a digital font inspired by a traditional engraved typeface. It all started with some visual and historical research into the reference font. Then came the hand-drawn sketches — working out proportions, the balance between thick and thin strokes, and defining the distinctive character of each letter. Those drawings were then digitised and turned into vector outlines, with careful attention paid to the construction of every glyph. After that, I worked on the spacing and kerning to make sure the font reads smoothly and feels balanced. The project wrapped up with the design of a type specimen, created to show off the font’s formal qualities and explore how it could be used in different contexts.
